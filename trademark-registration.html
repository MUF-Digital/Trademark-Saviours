FAQs

Federal Trademark Registration

  • What if you found a trademark similar to yours? Can it still be registered?

    Most of the time, the answer would be no as it will create confusion. However, if the mark is being used for other products or services then it may be possible because it will not create confusion among the customers.

  • Do the business name and logo have to be registered separately?

    Yes.

  • How many trademark classes need to be registered?

    This will depend on what you are selling. The USPTO has put all products and services into 45 classes. The trademark protection will be limited to the classes listed in the application you forward.

  • What time is required to register a trademark?

    Six to nine months in case the mark is used in commerce. The USPTO will be reviewing the application within a few months and then approve it or follow up with an office action sent to you with questions. Once published, if the public does not object within 30 days, then it will be officially registered. In case your mark is not being used in commerce then it will take longer.

  • Chances of the trademark being rejected?

    Yes, there are chances of rejection if the USPTO finds that the consumers would be confusing it with another trademark.
